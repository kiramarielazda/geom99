Our Solution (...for now)

Mapbox, GeoJSON & Leaflet
KoboToolbox Forms

Summary

After exploring several open-source web mapping options, we found that a combination of Mapbox, Leaflet and GeoJSON will help us create the best product for our client, the Friends of the Dumoine River. Mapbox's design functionality will allow us to completely customize a base map to include relevant labels, topographic features and land use classes, all of which are key elements of a quality trail map. Leaflet's JavaScript library has proven that it can support the functionality we would like to include on the map, such as popups, search bars and layer toggle options. After testing both GeoJSON and KML data, GeoJSON was the clear choice when it comes to accessibility and customization. At this time, we have successfully designed an interactive and open-source web map framework that will continue to be developed to meet the client's needs of showcasing the trail's information and unique features to potential hikers.

In exploring data collection solutions, we determined that KoBoToolbox would give the best suite of tools for our client. While there are other options available that would give greater design functionality and a more enterprise-based solution, at the scale which the Friends of the Dumoine River is operating, this service will give them accessibility to the data they wish to collect and distribute as appropriate. Currently, we have working drafts for both the products that have been requested, open-source versions of both a trail maintenance form and an interesting location form, and they will be improved upon based on our client's feedback to ensure that they are collecting all relevant data.

Future Steps

Moving forward, we plan to dedicate significant effort to designing a suitable base map in Mapbox. We have just barely scratched the surface of what Mapbox Studio has to offer, but we are confident that we will be able to produce an impressive result once we have more time to explore the interface. Also, the contents and design of the map popups will be significantly developed once we have collected and received all relevant information the client would like to include on the map. Further, other capabilities of Leaflet will most definitely be discovered and integrated into our web map to make it a dynamic experience for users.

Our continuing development of the surveys will include more exploration into the analysis of the results. KoBoToolbox is home to many integrated tools for data exploration and visualization, and our intention is to be knowledgeable on how to include entries from these forms into the final web map product for the client. We will also be evaluating more complex design characteristics to design a visually appealing form for the Friends.
